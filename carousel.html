28 Valentine's Day Outfit Ideas for Fashion Girls

Celebrating Valentine's Day this year will, like everything else, be different. Depending on where you live, dining out may not be an option. Or going to a romantic movie. But even if you have to spend the night at home, you can still dress to impress. And if you’re fashion-minded, your Valentine’s Day outfit has to be equal parts sexy and stylish.

So we went ahead and put together some stellar Valentine's Day outfit ideas. They come courtesy of our favorite street style stars and Instagram influencers. Looking for a lingerie-based ensemble you may only feel comfortable wearing in the privacy of your own home? Or perhaps you want to learn layering tricks so you can still sport that slinky dress while safely picking up takeout. Regardless, we’ve got the answers. Just be prepared for a few alterations, like slipping on tights or draping a heavy coat on top if you decide to leave your humble abode. Because February tends to be the most frigid month on the calendar.

Make a classic black suit Valentine's Day-appropriate by slipping on a red striped blouse. A red lip and strappy sandals pull everything together.

Forget red and pink. Layer a peach shirt under a check blazer and matching skirt. Stark heels round out the look.

Take that lacy red bustier and combine it with baggy pink pants.

Style a pink cardigan with a blush-colored printed maxi skirt. Polish off the outfit with gold jewelry.

Slip a creamy turtleneck under a silky slip dress in a reddish shade for a cozy yet sexy look.

Patent leather really makes an impression. Grab a pair of overalls or a coordinating top and pants, then top things off with a reddish-brown cardigan. Work in strappy sandals or boots to complete the ensemble.

Make a black midi dress Valentine’s Day-ready with mismatched ankle boots. Red and white recommended, but a red and pink pairing also works.

All you really need is a red turtleneck to spice things up. Black leather pants, boots and a long coat amp up the look. So do statement earrings.

You can wear a basic top and miniskirt as long as you cover them up with a deep red blazer. Two-tone boots make it less business and more play.

A single strip of red on a crisp white shirt serves as a subtle nod to the "holiday." Team it with a purple vest and pants plus embellished pumps.

Try teaming an oxblood sweater with a pleated orange skirt for the big night. Tie the pieces together with a dark red belt.

Make your favorite black dress Valentine’s Day-appropriate by wrapping it up with a red belt.

February is still leather weather so take things to the extreme by combining a red jacket, black shirt and pink skirt.

Take a subtler approach with a red and white striped top and a flowy ivory skirt. Lace-up ankle boots FTW.

A pair of rhinestone-waisted pants is always a good idea. Style them with a red cropped top and slip on a set of sparkly cuffs over your sleeves.

Top off a black fringed dress with a red animal print blazer. Just add in sock sneakers or boots.

Dress up a tan polo sweater with a long burgundy skirt and complementary knee-high boots.

Layer a black turtleneck under a striped button-down, then tuck both into bright red pants. Match your bag to your top half.

A magenta dress really makes an impression layered over a turtleneck in a similar shade. Don’t forget the red velvet boots.

Rock a camel-colored leather jacket as a shirt and tuck it into a red pleated skirt. Red accessories really heat things up.

Keep comfy by pairing a black jumpsuit with a pink blazer and red boots.

Go halfsies with a red and light pink blouse teamed with a red hot pleated skirt.

Break up a red turtleneck and red and black pants with a charcoal gray coat.

Blue tweed goes surprisingly well with a maroon sweater, wine-colored tights and coordinating platform sandals.

A black top and a red plaid skirt is one killer combo. Drape a classic camel coat over your shoulders and carry a crimson quilted bag.

Style red pants and red sandals with a neon green top and a plaid blazer.

Instead of obvious floral patterned pieces, try watercolor versions. Keep everything else neutral, from your sweater to your boots.

Try a more laid-back approach to date-night dressing by pairing a silky fuchsia top with red pants.
